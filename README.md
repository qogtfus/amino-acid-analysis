## The Real Problem: Why Muscle Slips Away After 40

There's a word for age-related muscle loss: sarcopenia. And it's not subtle.

Starting around age 30, people who don't actively fight it lose roughly 3-5% of muscle mass per decade. By 50, the decline accelerates. By 70, some people have lost up to 30% of their peak muscle.

Why does this happen? Two main reasons.

First, the body becomes less responsive to dietary protein. This is called anabolic resistance. You eat protein, but your muscles don't use it as efficiently to rebuild themselves.

Second, many people eat less protein as they age — appetite drops, cooking feels like a chore, or they focus on "lighter" meals and unknowingly under-dose on amino acids.

This is where amino acid supplements like Advanced Amino Formula enter the picture. They don't replace food. But they provide a concentrated dose of the specific building blocks muscle tissue needs — in a form that sidesteps the digestion problem.
